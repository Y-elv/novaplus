Our Veterinary Services

At Novaplus Veterinary, we provide a wide range of veterinary services to keep your pets healthy and happy. Our experienced veterinarians and staff are dedicated to delivering the highest standard of care in a compassionate and friendly environment.

Preventive Care

Regular preventive care is essential for maintaining your pet's health and detecting potential issues before they become serious. Our preventive services include:

Vaccinations

Core and non-core vaccinations to protect against common diseases

Wellness Exams

Comprehensive physical examinations to assess overall health

Parasite Control

Prevention and treatment for fleas, ticks, heartworms, and intestinal parasites

Dental Care

Dental cleanings, oral exams, and home care recommendations

Nutritional Counseling

Personalized diet plans and nutritional advice for your pet's needs

Emergency Care

We provide emergency veterinary care during our regular business hours. For after-hours emergencies, please contact our emergency line.

Emergency Contact

Signs Your Pet Needs Emergency Care:

  • Difficulty breathing or excessive coughing
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Seizures or loss of consciousness
  • Bloated or distended abdomen
  • Inability to urinate or defecate
  • Ingestion of toxic substances
  • Sudden paralysis or inability to stand

Frequently Asked Questions

We recommend annual wellness exams for most adult pets. Puppies, kittens, senior pets, and pets with health conditions may need more frequent visits. Your veterinarian will recommend the best schedule for your pet's specific needs.

Core vaccinations for dogs typically include rabies, distemper, parvovirus, and adenovirus. For cats, core vaccines are rabies, feline distemper, and feline herpesvirus. Additional vaccines may be recommended based on your pet's lifestyle and risk factors.

Pets often hide pain, but signs may include decreased activity, reluctance to jump or climb stairs, decreased appetite, excessive licking of a body part, changes in behavior, or vocalizing. If you suspect your pet is in pain, please contact us.

If it's during our regular hours, call us immediately at (555) 123-4567. For after-hours emergencies, please call our emergency line. If your pet is experiencing severe trauma, difficulty breathing, seizures, or other life-threatening conditions, bring them in immediately.

We accept various payment methods including cash, credit cards, and pet insurance. We also work with third-party financing options like CareCredit. Please contact us for more information about payment options.
